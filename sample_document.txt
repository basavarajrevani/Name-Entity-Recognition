Apple Inc. is an American multinational technology company headquartered in Cupertino, California. 
The company was founded by Steve Jobs, Steve Wozniak, and Ronald Wayne on April 1, 1976.

Tim Cook became the CEO in August 2011, succeeding Steve Jobs who passed away in October 2011.
Apple reported revenue of $394.3 billion in fiscal year 2022.

The company's main products include:
- iPhone: Released in 2007, revolutionized the smartphone industry
- iPad: Launched in 2010, created the tablet market
- Mac computers: Including MacBook Pro, MacBook Air, and iMac
- Apple Watch: Introduced in 2015, became the world's best-selling smartwatch

Apple's headquarters, known as Apple Park, opened in Cupertino in 2017.
The company employs over 164,000 people worldwide as of 2022.

Major competitors include Samsung, Google, Microsoft, and Amazon.
Apple's stock trades on NASDAQ under the ticker symbol AAPL.
